Spain 1808: The Misjudgment of an Asymmetric Adversary
SYNTHESIZED CONTEXT — drawn from the Bayonne abdications (May 1808), the
Correspondance with Joseph and Murat during 1808–1813, contemporary reports
from French commanders in Spain (Suchet, Soult), and Tulard and Esdaile's
analyses of the Peninsular War.

== THE PROBLEM AS HE FRAMED IT ==

By 1808 Spain was the last continental hole in the Continental System: its
ports were nominally closed but smuggling was tolerated, its government
under Charles IV and Manuel Godoy was an unreliable ally, and his troops
already occupied much of the country to enforce blockade compliance against
Portugal. He saw an opportunity: the Spanish Bourbons were unpopular, the
royal family was quarreling publicly, and a regime change administered by
him could install a reliable Bonaparte (his brother Joseph) and resolve the
peninsula problem permanently.

== THE FRAMING DECISION ==

He summoned both Charles IV and his son Ferdinand VII to Bayonne under
diplomatic pretext, then forced both to abdicate, transferred the Spanish
crown to Joseph, and announced the change as a fait accompli. The framing
was that Spain's people would welcome a modernizing king imposed by the
revolutionary order, that the existing church-state-aristocracy structure
was so corrupt that anyone replacing it would be greeted as liberation, and
that the resistance would be limited to court factions easily neutralized.

== THE PRINCIPLE THAT FAILED ==

The construct that had worked in Italy and the Rhineland — install a
modernizing French-aligned regime, paid for by abolishing feudal privilege,
enforced by a French army garrison — assumed a population that would
correctly identify its long-term interest with reform. In Spain, Catholic
peasant identity, inherited dynastic loyalty, and immediate humiliation by
foreign conquest produced a population that fought back at every
administrative scale: regional juntas, urban risings (Madrid Dos de Mayo),
guerrilla bands that attacked supply lines, and a regular army that
refused to disperse. The same demographic that had requisitioned cooperatively
for him in Bavaria, Italy, and Saxony chose costly insurrection in Spain.

== THE PRINCIPLE'S COST ==

Spain consumed 250,000–300,000 French troops continuously from 1808 to 1814,
killed roughly 200,000 of them, and never produced the strategic peace the
campaign was supposed to deliver. He called it "my Spanish ulcer" — a wound
that could not be closed and bled continuously, draining the resources
needed for the Russia campaign and the German campaigns afterward. The
strategic mistake was not the invasion but the framing: Spain was treated
as a problem solvable by regime change applied from above, when it was a
problem of popular sovereignty that required genuine consent he could not
manufacture.

== THE PATTERN OF NOT REVISING ==

After Bailén (July 1808), where a French army surrendered to Spanish forces
— the first such surrender of his reign — he chose escalation over
withdrawal. He led 200,000 troops personally into Spain in late 1808,
restored Joseph in Madrid, then left for Austria in early 1809 without
having defeated the guerrilla apparatus. The decision pattern is consistent
with Russia 1812: when a campaign fails to deliver its intended effect, he
escalates rather than reframes, because his operating method has no procedure
for "this approach is wrong, change category."

== POST-DECISION REASONING ==

On Saint Helena he conceded that "the unfortunate war in Spain ruined me."
The retrospective lucidity is real but did not appear at the time. The
construct that had worked in dozens of campaigns proved category-specific,
and he could not see the category boundary until after the cost was paid.

== WHAT THIS REVEALS ABOUT THE OPERATING METHOD ==

Spain reveals the boundary of his strongest constructs: deception works
against opposing generals, not against insurgents who refuse to constitute
a force he can outmaneuver; reform works for populations whose elite has
been delegitimized, not for populations whose religious-dynastic identity
is the legitimacy. The operating method assumes opponents who can be
out-thought; it has no theory of opponents who simply refuse to play in
the dimension where his thinking operates.
