Pierre Curie by Marie Curie

Curated excerpt for the Marie Curie framework.

Marie Curie describes how she and Pierre treated scientific work as a shared discipline of exact measurement, patient repetition, and restraint in interpretation. She emphasizes that anomalous data should be followed rather than dismissed, and that the radium work demanded persistence through exhausting conditions because the measurements mattered more than convenience. The memoir also records their decision to publish the extraction methods openly rather than treat the discovery as private property.
