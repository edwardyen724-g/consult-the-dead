## Three Decision Frameworks

### 1. Productivity & Management Overhead (Machiavelli)
The hire should happen when marginal employee productivity exceeds management overhead. This is not when you're "too busy" — it's when delegation has been attempted and labor is the manifest bottleneck.

### 2. Leadership Readiness (Marcus Aurelius)
The hire should happen when you are prepared to be a genuine leader — when you have attempted to clarify your thinking, document decisions, and can be the leader this person needs.

### 3. Evidence-Based Hiring (Marie Curie)
The hire should happen when empirical evidence shows you have 15-20+ hours per week of clearly definable, delegable work that is blocked by lack of hands.

## Debate Structure (3 Rounds, Full Framework)

Round 1: Each thinker presents their primary framework
- Machiavelli: Productivity and delegation readiness
- Aurelius: Leadership character and readiness
- Curie: Empirical evidence and data-driven hiring

Round 2: Each responds to the others' frameworks
- Machiavelli acknowledges character matters but doesn't require perfect readiness
- Aurelius acknowledges growth comes through action, not waiting
- Curie sharpens the empirical threshold with nuance from colleagues

Round 3: Convergence toward actionable decision
- All three move toward: hire when evidence, leadership readiness, and productivity bottleneck align
- Each holds firm on their specific dimension while acknowledging the others' contributions
- Final framing: the first hire is a hypothesis to be tested with evidence
