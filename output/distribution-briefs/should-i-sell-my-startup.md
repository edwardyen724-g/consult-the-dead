# Distribution Brief: Should I Sell My Startup?

## Campaign Overview

**Decision Page**: Should I Sell My Startup?
**Target Segment**: Founders in acquisition discussions or seriously evaluating exit options
**Primary Intent**: High-commercial-intent decision-stage founders seeking validation/framework for exit decision
**Campaign Duration**: 6 weeks (Phase 1 launch + Wave X follow-up)
**Success Metric**: 40+ agon sessions from targeted outreach; 10%+ conversion to Pro from this funnel

---

## Wave X Outreach Strategy

### Audience Definition

**Primary (Tier 1 - Highest Commercial Intent)**
- Founders with exits in discussion (identifiable via CrunchBase, AngelList recent funding rounds showing multiple acquirers, or LinkedIn signals)
- Founders who have publicly discussed acquisition interest
- Founders with 3-5 years operating history (more likely to consider exit)
- Count: ~40-50 founders with verified email

**Secondary (Tier 2 - Intent Indicators)**
- Founders who have recently raised seed/Series A (2-3 year timeline to potential exit)
- Founders with exit-stage metrics (strong retention, defensible positioning)
- Investors/advisors who advise on exit decisions
- Count: ~50-75 people

### Outreach Vehicles (Wave X)

1. **Personalized Email** (Tier 1 only)
   - Subject line: "For [Founder Name]: The exit decision you haven't thought through yet"
   - Body: 3-4 paragraph custom note + agon link with UTM params
   - Send window: Tuesdays-Thursdays, 9am-11am local time
   - Cadence: Single send (no resends in Wave X)
   - Expected response rate: 5-8% (opens), 1-2% (clicks)

2. **LinkedIn Comment/Message** (Tier 2, if no verified email)
   - Trigger: Founder posts about fundraising, hiring, or strategy
   - Message: Short comment showing agon framework, DM link
   - Tone: Peer-to-peer, not salesy
   - Cadence: 2-3 comments per week over 4 weeks

3. **Warm Intro via Advisor Network** (Tier 1, selective)
   - Leverage intros from investors, accelerators, or advisory board members
   - Intro: "You should hear what [Founder] is building — a debate framework for exit decisions"
   - Expected response rate: 10-15% (positive reception)

4. **Community Mentions** (Organic, ongoing)
   - HN: Post to discussion threads about exits, acquisitions
   - Reddit r/startups: Comment in threads about "when to sell"
   - Indie Hackers: Post in discussions about founder exits
   - Tone: Educational, not promotional

## Messaging Matrix

### For Founders in Active Discussions
**Problem**: "You have an offer. Or offers. But you don't know what really matters — the valuation, the control you're trading, the strength of your position, or what you'll do next."
**Solution**: "Three frameworks show you what each variable actually means and why the three of them sometimes conflict."
**Proof**: "Machiavelli says X, Aurelius says Y, Sun Tzu says Z — and they're each right about something different."

### For Advisors / Investors
**Problem**: "When a founder you advise asks 'should I sell?' the conversation defaults to financial analysis. But the real decision is deeper."
**Solution**: "A framework that brings three perspectives — power, duty, and strategy — into conversation."
**Proof**: "Useful for walking founders through what they actually need to think about before deciding."

### For General Audience (Later Phases)
**Problem**: "Exit decisions are usually framed as yes/no. But they're really about three conflicting dimensions: optionality, stewardship, and terrain."
**Solution**: "A debate that shows all three perspectives so you can decide which one matters most to you."
**Proof**: "Frameworks from historical strategists who understand decision-making under uncertainty."

## Risk Mitigation

**Risk**: Low open rate on email outreach
**Mitigation**: A/B test subject lines on first 10 emails; pivot to LinkedIn if email stalls

**Risk**: High bounce rate (prospects no longer at company)
**Mitigation**: Use current company/role validation tools; prioritize LinkedIn outreach for harder-to-find contacts

**Risk**: Low conversion to Pro despite agon engagement
**Mitigation**: Monitor session behavior; test different CTA placement/messaging; consider drip email follow-up for non-converters

**Risk**: Agon session abandonment (low completion rate)
**Mitigation**: Monitor drop-off points; ensure 3 full council rounds are published before outreach begins
